Part I Writeup

Police Siren

A browser implementation of Farnell's police-siren design using a logarithmic sweep oscillator, horn-style distortion and filtering, and a small network of environmental echoes.

Mode

Ready. Pick a mode, then start the siren.

Signal Chain

  • A custom logarithmic waveform acts as both the LFO and the audio oscillator.
  • The LFO sweeps the siren oscillator from roughly 300 Hz to 1100 Hz.
  • Strong clipping simulates the overdriven diaphragm in the horn.
  • A band-pass filter centered near 1.5 kHz shapes the horn resonance.
  • Three recirculating delays create building reflections and city echo.

Implementation Notes

The book later points out that a triangle wave gives a very similar spectrum, but this version keeps the spirit of the earlier section by sampling a piecewise exponential charge/discharge waveform. The result is then processed by the horn and echo stages to get the bright, resonant siren character.

Mode Guide

Wail uses the slower 0.1 Hz sweep from the text. It sounds broader and more distant. Yelp switches to a 3 Hz sweep for the urgent fast-cycle version. You can change modes while the siren is running.
